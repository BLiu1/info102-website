Cool Fact!

I should confess that I rushed to my Cool Fact/Stat because it sounded so odd to begin with. By the time I presented my cool fact/stat, I realized that it was not appropriate for the course. Nevertheless, I covered a joke programming language called C+= or "C Plus Equality". It was created by a group of satirists who exaggerated feminist stereotypes in a negative light.

Summary and Reflection

In lecture and through readings, we have discovered the benefits of having a diverse group working on a project. The ushering in of different opinions and non standard ways of thinking contributes to overall success. In a similar context, HASTAC user Ari Schlesinger proposed making a "feminist programming language" that should be so non-standard way as "to encourage and allow new ways of thinking about problems such that we can code using a feminist ideology." As a reaction to this, a satirical group calling themselves the "Feminist Software Foundation" put together a language specification with references to negative feminist stereotypes, mockery of rape and trigger warnings, and transphobia in it. Some feminists took this sarcasm the wrong way (e.g., blogger Molly White) and confused the satire as a sincere mockery of feminism.

There is a lesson to be learned here about mistaking satire for a sincere expression of the parodied views. As some people on the internet have figured out, a parody without an indication of satire will always draw some misunderstanding. Even with this in mind, some views are best not satirized, due to the extreme beliefs of some of the following's members. Case in point: the comment thread of Molly White's blog post. It is important to approach sensitive issues such as the feminist movement as neutral as possible to avoid stirring up senseless arguments.
