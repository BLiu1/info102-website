Transcript and Notes

Ada Lovelace is recognized by many as the first computer programmer for working with inventor Charles Babbage on what is now considered the first prototype of a modern computer, the Analytical Engine. Translating an Italian engineer's paper on Babbage's Analytical Engine, Lovelace added notes that amounted to three times the length of the translation. The notes contain the most well-thought-out and documented instructions for calculating a number series for the Analytical Engine and predictions of the many uses of such a machine.
